This document contains an important information on the class content, homeworks, etc. Please read it!

Goals: This course serves as an introduction to Commutative algebra with a discussion of category theory and a brief discussion of connections to Algebraic geometry. This document also contains a list of references. There is no required textbook.

Grading: Based on 6 homework sets (60%) and a take-home final exam (40%). Due dates for homeworks: Sept 23, Oct 7, Oct 25, Nov 4, Nov 18, Dec 9, by the end of day.

Rules: For homework sets, peer discussion is allowed but only after your own thinking; you are also required to write your solution on your own, if it (partially) comes from a discussion. Homework sets and the take-home final should be submitted via Canvas. You are encouraged to texify your work, but scans of handwriting are also acceptable. Overdue submissions will not be accepted.

    • Schedule:

  • Sept 2, Lecture 1: Basics on rings, homomorphisms and ideals. Notes. Bonus: Non-commutative counterparts, I.
  • Sept 7, Lecture 2: Wrap-up on quotient rings. Operations on ideals. Maximal ideals. Notes. Bonus: Non-commutative counterparts, II.
  • Sept 9, Lecture 3: Prime ideals. Modules and their homomorphisms. Constructions with modules. Notes. Bonus: Non-commutative counterparts, III.
  • Sept 14, Lecture 4: Submodules and quotient modules. Finitely generated and free modules. Notes (including an example of a non-finitely generated submodule in a finitely generated module that we didn't have time to cover in class).
  • Sept 16, Lecture 5: Noetherian rings and modules. Hilbert basis theorem. Noetherian modules over Noetherian rings. Notes. Bonuses: Non-Noetherian rings in Complex Analysis. Why Hilbert cared.
  • Sept 21, Lecture 6: More on Noetherian modules. Artinian modules and rings. Finite length modules. Notes.
  • Sept 23, Lecture 7: Principal ideal domains and modules over them. Notes. Bonus: modules over C[x,y].
  • Sept 28, Lecture 8: Continuation of the proof of the main theorem from Lecture 7. Brief discussion of integral extensions of rings. Notes.
  • Sept 30, Lecture 9: Integral extensions and closures of rings. Notes (minor update on Oct 2).
  • Oct 5, Lecture 10: Fraction fields, computation of integral closures, and normal domains. Multiplicative subsets. Notes
  • Oct 7, Lecture 11: Localization of rings and modules. Notes. Bonus: localization in noncommutative rings.
  • Oct 12, Lecture 12: Localization of modules, continued. Local rings. Notes.
  • Oct 14, Lecture 13: Categories and functors. Notes. Bonus: Homotopy category of topological spaces.
  • Oct 19, Lecture 14: Functors, continued. Functor morphisms. Notes. Bonus: category equivalences.
  • Oct 26, Lecture 15: The Yoneda lemma. Objects representing functors. Notes.
  • Oct 28, Lecture 16: Products and coproducts in categories. Tensor products of modules. Notes.
  • Nov 2, Lecture 17: Tensor products of modules, cont'd. Notes.
  • Nov 4, Lecture 18: Tensor products of modules, finished. Tensor product of algebras. Adjoint functors. Notes.
  • Nov 9, Lecture 19: Adjoint functors, cont'd. Tensor-Hom adjunction. Notes. Bonuses: uniqueness of adjoints (revised 11/17), analogy with linear maps, adjunction unit and counit.
  • Nov 11, Lecture 20: Tensor-Hom adjunction finished. Additive functors and exactness. Notes, modified 11/21. Bonuses: Additive and abelian categories.
  • Nov 16, Lecture 21: Properties of exact functors. Localization as base change. Projective and flat modules. Notes, modified 11/19. Bonuses: 5-lemma, injective modules.
  • Nov 18, Lecture 22: Hilbert's Nullstellensatz. Notes. Bonus: Why Hilbert cared.
  • Nov 30, Lecture 23: Algebraic subsets vs radical ideals. Prime ideals and irreducibility. Notes. Bonuses: Is this ideal radical? Tensor product of algebras vs product of algebraic subsets.
  • Dec 2, Lecture 24: Algebra homomorphisms vs polynomial maps. Geometric significance of localization. Preliminary version of notes. Updated notes (with Section 1.3 expanded and Section 2.1 slightly expanded). Bonuses: What is an algebraic variety? Projective varieties.
  • Dec 7, Lecture 25: Modules, geometrically. Modules over local rings and the Nakayma lemma. Notes.
  • Dec 9, Lecture 26, a.k.a. the last: Projective modules vs locally free modules. Notes.
